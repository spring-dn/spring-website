This page lists the 15 SPRING Doctoral Candidate (DC) projects. Each DC focuses on a specific
aspect of resilience in large-scale critical infrastructures and is hosted by one of the
beneficiary institutions.

<div class="cards-grid">
  <div class="card">
    <h3>DC1 – Physics-informed adversarial robustness of network models</h3>
    <p class="card-meta">
      Host: Universität Bielefeld (UNIBI)
    </p>
    <p>
      Investigate adversarial attacks that respect network dynamics and physical constraints, and
      develop physics-informed learning and generative models to improve the robustness of AI
      components used in water and energy infrastructures.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="card">
    <h3>DC2 – Diffusive dynamics for self-adaptable supply networks</h3>
    <p class="card-meta">
      Host: Université d’Orléans (UOR)
    </p>
    <p>
      Develop a theory of diffusive dynamics for systems of cyber-physical systems and use it to
      design resilient supply networks in water and energy domains that can absorb local
      intrusions and disturbances.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="card">
    <h3>DC3 – Contracts and dynamic reconfiguration for resilient infrastructures</h3>
    <p class="card-meta">
      Host: Université d’Orléans (UOR)
    </p>
    <p>
      Combine model learning with quantitative contracts to support automatic detection of
      configuration problems and demand-driven dynamic reconfiguration of critical infrastructures
      without sacrificing safety.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="card">
    <h3>DC4 – Formal patterns for allostasis-like reconfiguration</h3>
    <p class="card-meta">
      Host: University of Oslo (UiO)
    </p>
    <p>
      Use formal methods and design patterns to realise allostasis-like behaviour in critical
      infrastructures, allowing systems to reconfigure safely and proactively in response to
      evolving threats and disruptions.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="card">
    <h3>DC5 – Explaining complex drift phenomena in networked data</h3>
    <p class="card-meta">
      Host: Universität Bielefeld (UNIBI)
    </p>
    <p>
      Develop explainable AI methods to detect and interpret concept drift and emerging anomalies
      in networked data streams, enabling human operators to inspect and understand new failure or
      attack patterns in water and transport applications.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="card">
    <h3>DC6 – Explaining agent decisions in lifelong machine learning</h3>
    <p class="card-meta">
      Host: Eight Bells (8BEL), PhD enrolment at University of Cyprus (UCY)
    </p>
    <p>
      Design explainable, lifelong learning approaches that keep track of and explain evolving
      decision policies of intelligent agents, with a focus on trust in AI-driven decisions for
      water and energy infrastructures.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="card">
    <h3>DC7 – Correlation modelling for distributed smart supplies</h3>
    <p class="card-meta">
      Host: SingularLogic (SLG), collaboration with University of Oldenburg (UOL)
    </p>
    <p>
      Develop correlation models and analysis methods for distributed smart energy supplies, to
      strengthen monitoring and support the diagnosis of incidents and abnormal behaviour in
      smart grids.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="card">
    <h3>DC8 – Trust state assessment in smart energy systems</h3>
    <p class="card-meta">
      Host: SingularLogic (SLG), collaboration with University of Oldenburg (UOL)
    </p>
    <p>
      Build multi-faceted models of “trust state” for distributed smart supplies that combine
      data-driven indicators and system knowledge, improving vulnerability detection and isolation
      in energy networks.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="card">
    <h3>DC9 – Anomaly detection in multi-agent traffic scenarios</h3>
    <p class="card-meta">
      Host: Humatects (HMT), PhD enrolment at University of Oldenburg (UOL)
    </p>
    <p>
      Design methods for detecting anomalies and critical or malicious driving manoeuvres in
      multi-agent traffic scenarios, using data from connected and automated vehicles and
      intelligent transport systems.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="card">
    <h3>DC10 – Moving-target defence in networked switched systems</h3>
    <p class="card-meta">
      Host: Université d’Orléans (UOR)
    </p>
    <p>
      Develop moving-target defence strategies for networked switched systems to strengthen the
      diagnosis of stealthy cyber-attacks, especially in energy and transport infrastructures,
      while maintaining system performance.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="card">
    <h3>DC11 – Distributed incident response for water and energy systems</h3>
    <p class="card-meta">
      Host: University of Cyprus (UCY)
    </p>
    <p>
      Derive distributed response principles for systems of cyber-physical systems in water and
      energy, supporting coordinated incident response that balances resilience, safety and
      service quality.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="card">
    <h3>DC12 – Cyber-physical agent-based digital twins</h3>
    <p class="card-meta">
      Host: University of Cyprus (UCY)
    </p>
    <p>
      Develop cyber-physical, agent-based digital twins for situational awareness and decision
      support, enabling operators to explore, predict and manage incident scenarios in water,
      energy and transport infrastructures.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="card">
    <h3>DC13 – Contract-based reconfiguration in transportation</h3>
    <p class="card-meta">
      Host: University of Oldenburg (UOL)
    </p>
    <p>
      Apply contract-based design and compositional reasoning to support safe reconfiguration and
      incident response in transportation infrastructures, linking formal assurances with
      operational decision-making.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="card">
    <h3>DC14 – Robust shielding for safety-critical ML systems</h3>
    <p class="card-meta">
      Host: Graz University of Technology (TUG)
    </p>
    <p>
      Enhance shielding mechanisms for learning-enabled controllers so that safety and
      performance are preserved under disturbances, faults and partial observability, with a
      focus on transportation use cases.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="card">
    <h3>DC15 – Explaining shielding decisions in transportation</h3>
    <p class="card-meta">
      Host: Graz University of Technology (TUG)
    </p>
    <p>
      Develop techniques to compute and present explainable shields, representing safety
      mechanisms as compact, interpretable structures and assessing their usefulness through
      user studies in transportation scenarios.
    </p>
  </div>
</div>
