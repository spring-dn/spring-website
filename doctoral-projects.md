This page lists the 15 SPRING Doctoral Candidate (DC) projects. Each DC focuses on a specific
aspect of resilience in large-scale critical infrastructures and is hosted by one of the
beneficiary institutions.

<div class="cards-grid">
  <div class="card">
    <h3>DC1 – Physics-informed adversarial robustness of network models</h3>
    <p class="card-meta">
      Host: Universität Bielefeld (UNIBI)
    </p>
    <p>
      Study adversarial attacks that are consistent with network dynamics and physical constraints,
      and develop physics-informed learning and generative models to improve the robustness of AI
      used in water and transport infrastructures.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="card">
    <h3>DC2 – Increasing self-adaptability through generalizations of diffusive dynamics in SoCPSs</h3>
    <p class="card-meta">
      Host: Université d’Orléans (UOR)
    </p>
    <p>
      Systematically develop a theory of diffusive dynamics for systems of cyber-physical systems
      and exploit it to design and analyse resilient supply networks in water and energy domains.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="card">
    <h3>DC3 – Secure distributed set-based state estimation in networked switched systems</h3>
    <p class="card-meta">
      Host: Université d’Orléans (UOR)
    </p>
    <p>
      Design secure, set-based distributed observers that can reconstruct system states under cyber
      and physical attacks, increasing the resilience of networked water–energy systems.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="card">
    <h3>DC4 – Formalizing resilience and engineering provably resilient-by-design SoCPSs</h3>
    <p class="card-meta">
      Host: University of Oslo (UiO)
    </p>
    <p>
      Develop formal specification and verification methods, and libraries of formal design patterns,
      to engineer systems of cyber-physical systems that are provably resilient by design.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="card">
    <h3>DC5 – Explaining complex drift phenomena in networked data</h3>
    <p class="card-meta">
      Host: Universität Bielefeld (UNIBI)
    </p>
    <p>
      Create explainable AI techniques to detect and interpret complex drift and emerging risks in
      networked data, and transfer them to water distribution and smart energy management use cases.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="card">
    <h3>DC6 – Explaining agent decisions in a Lifelong Machine Learning framework</h3>
    <p class="card-meta">
      Host: Eight Bells Ltd (8BEL)
    </p>
    <p>
      Develop a lifelong learning framework with built-in explanation mechanisms for autonomous
      agents, targeting energy-efficient training and trusted AI-based decision-making in smart
      water and energy supplies.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="card">
    <h3>DC7 – Correlation modelling and analysis for distributed smart supplies</h3>
    <p class="card-meta">
      Host: SingularLogic S.A. (SLG)
    </p>
    <p>
      Build data repositories and correlation models for distributed smart energy supplies to
      support privacy-preserving analysis, incident detection and resilience assessment in power
      systems.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="card">
    <h3>DC8 – Multi-facetted trust state assessment for distributed smart supplies</h3>
    <p class="card-meta">
      Host: SingularLogic S.A. (SLG)
    </p>
    <p>
      Extend multi-facetted trust assessment methods for digitalised power systems by including
      hardware-related facets, enabling holistic, traceable trust state estimation for smart
      energy networks.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="card">
    <h3>DC9 – Anomaly detection in multi-agent traffic scenarios</h3>
    <p class="card-meta">
      Host: Humatects GmbH (HMT)
    </p>
    <p>
      Investigate cooperative long-term behaviour prediction and anomaly detection in multi-agent
      traffic using simulators and V2X communication, targeting critical and malicious driving
      scenarios for resilient smart transport.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="card">
    <h3>DC10 – Stealthy attacks diagnosis in networked switched systems</h3>
    <p class="card-meta">
      Host: Université d’Orléans (UOR)
    </p>
    <p>
      Combine moving-target defence with distributed set-based observers to detect and isolate
      stealthy cyber-physical attacks in large-scale switched systems, with applications to smart
      grids and transport.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="card">
    <h3>DC11 – Distributed multi-CPS resilience response principles</h3>
    <p class="card-meta">
      Host: University of Cyprus (UCY)
    </p>
    <p>
      Develop modelling and simulation frameworks, based on semantic control graphs, to understand
      interdependencies in water–energy cyber-physical infrastructures and to design distributed
      resilience response strategies.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="card">
    <h3>DC12 – A Cyber-Physical Agent-Based Digital Twin Architecture for Resilient Critical Infrastructure Services</h3>
    <p class="card-meta">
      Host: University of Cyprus (UCY)
    </p>
    <p>
      Design an agent-based digital twin architecture that integrates formal methods, simulation
      tools and AI (including large language models) to support anticipatory self-adaptation and
      risk-aware incident response in critical infrastructures.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="card">
    <h3>DC13 – Model-based testing and contract-based synthesis for resilience of networked CPS</h3>
    <p class="card-meta">
      Host: Carl von Ossietzky Universität Oldenburg (UOL)
    </p>
    <p>
      Combine model learning, model-based testing and contract-based reasoning to automatically
      generate safe, security-enhancing reconfigurations for networked automotive and transport
      systems.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="card">
    <h3>DC14 – Robust shielding mechanisms to guarantee safety & performance for safety-critical machine-learning systems</h3>
    <p class="card-meta">
      Host: Graz University of Technology (TUG)
    </p>
    <p>
      Extend shielding techniques for learning-enabled controllers using robust and partially
      observable models, ensuring safety and basic performance for safety-critical ML systems in
      transportation.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="card">
    <h3>DC15 – Explaining Shielding Decisions</h3>
    <p class="card-meta">
      Host: Graz University of Technology (TUG)
    </p>
    <p>
      Develop algorithms and tools to compute compact, explainable shields—e.g. decision-tree
      representations—and evaluate their understandability for safety-critical applications in
      smart transportation.
    </p>
  </div>
</div>

<p>
Each doctoral project combines methodological research with case studies and secondments in water,
energy and transport infrastructures. Detailed descriptions, eligibility criteria and application
procedures will be published with the recruitment call.
</p>
